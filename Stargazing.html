Stargazing Mondays

Mondays, MT Week 1 to 2: 7:30 to 8:30 PM,
Week 3 to 8: 6:30 to 7:30 PM (If the weather permits)


Join us in front of Martin Wood Lecture Theatre for an evening of observing the night sky! Our experts will guide you throughout the event as we explore the prominent stars, planets, constellations, and deep-sky objects. We will also keep an eye out for bright satellites like the International Space Station and the Hubble Space Telescope (yes, you read that correctly!) Given the interest, the event will be further expanded also to include imaging of the moon, planets, deep-sky objects and the night sky! It's a great opportunity for beginners and seasoned stargazers to get together and learn more about celestial objects. So come with your questions and curiosity, and connect with fellow space/astronomy enthusiasts!

Entry: The event is open to public and the entry is free!

Location: The open space in front of Martin Wood Lecture Theatre (see below)

Conditions: The event depends on cloud-free weather conditions. Please check our social media pages (Insta, FB) or the OUSAS Telescope Observations WhatsApp group for the latest updates about the event.
