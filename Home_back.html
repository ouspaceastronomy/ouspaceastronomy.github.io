Wishing you 
a Merry Christmas & a Happy New Year!


Looking due East, we see the Orion, Taurus, Auriga, Gemini, Lynx, and Cancer rising above the Christmas Tree in St John's Quad, the Chapel, the Great Tower, Muniment Tower and Founder’s Tower at the Magdalen College.

With a moderately lit foreground, the image was captured and processed as an HDR stack of about 500 images. Two sets of aperture, ISO, and exposure were required, one for the lights and one for capturing the stars.

Quick question: looking at the trails, how can we determine the latitude of Oxford?


The above image shows an hour-long journey of the Gemini, Cancer, Leo, Lynx, and Ursa Major above the Christmas Tree in the Front Quad of St Edmund Hall.

Capturing this image was more challenging as compared to the adjacent image given that the foreground was extremely bright with hundreds of lights shining directly into the lens and causing lens flares in the sky. To minimize contamination, different sets of aperture, ISO, and exposure were required for each type of the light source and for capturing the stars. The image is processed as an HDR stack (& not a composite) of over a thousand images.

For those who are keen: Since only the latest displays and only a few apps support HDR content, it is difficult to view the image at its full capacity. Open the website of OUSAS in Google Chrome or MS Edge on a 14" or 16" Macbook Pro to feel the image come to life.
Also tested on Google Chrome + Pixel 8/8 Pro phone.
